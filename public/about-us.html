Biography

With an illustrious 24-year career, Mr. Ramesh Nair is a distinguished Consultant Neurosurgeon renowned for expertise in Neurovascular and Skull Base Surgery. Trained in India, Japan, and the UK, his commitment to excellence is reflected in his qualifications—a Master of Surgery (MS), Diplomate of National Board (DNB) in General Surgery and Neurosurgery, a Master of Chirurgiae (MCh) in Neurosurgery, and Fellowships at institutions like Fujita Health University, Japan. He is also a Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons of Edinburgh (FRCSEd) and holds FRCS (S/N) qualifications.

Mr. Ramesh Nair's clinical journey spans Lumbar/Cervical Disc Disease management to precise Brain Tumour Surgeries, showcasing unwavering dedication to patient well-being. His surgical prowess and empathetic approach have earned respect from colleagues and patients alike, solidifying his reputation as a skilled and compassionate neurosurgeon.

Apart from clinical excellence, Mr. Ramesh Nair actively contributes to neurosurgery's advancement. Holding faculty roles at national and international conferences, he shares expertise, benefiting the global medical community and patients. Memberships in esteemed associations such as the British Medical Association (BMA), British Skull Base Society (SBNS), Skull Base Society, Neurovascular Society, and Indian Medical Association (IMA) underscore his commitment to professional excellence. Additionally, he is a Founder member and the current secretary of the International Academy of Neurosurgical Anatomy (IANA).

Through tireless efforts, Mr. Ramesh Nair continues to redefine neurosurgical care boundaries. His compassionate approach, coupled with a relentless pursuit of medical advancements, establishes him as a respected leader in neurosurgery.

Areas of Interest

Dr. Ramesh Nair is an actively engaged neurosurgeon, known for his involvement in cutting-edge clinical research trials. His work has been featured in esteemed peer-reviewed journals, including the Journal of the State Medical Society and Baylor University Medical Center's Proceedings.

In 2002, Dr. Ramesh Nair pursued a one-year fellowship in Skull Base and Neurovascular Surgery at Fujita Health University, Nagoya, Japan. In the UK, he received training at Oldchurch Hospital, London, and Charing Cross Hospital, London. After completing his formal training, he dedicated an additional 21 months to specialise in Skull Base and Neurovascular Surgery at Charing Cross Hospital. Before assuming his current role as a senior consultant neurosurgeon, he served as a locum consultant Neurosurgeon at John Radcliffe Infirmary, Oxford. Since 2010, he has been an integral part of the esteemed team at Imperial College Healthcare NHS Trust, offering his expertise as a skilled Skull Base and Neurovascular Surgeon.
